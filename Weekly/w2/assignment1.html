Website Critique

Poorly Designed Websites:

Welcome to the Space Jam

First up, Is the promotional Website for the 1996 cult classic film, Space Jam, starring Michael Jordan. The reason this site is so hilariously awful, is because of the cheap repeated pattern background, the tiny, ms paint looking icons, bold, cheesy looking cartoonish fonts, and overall horrible composition (The enormous amount of blank space on the page) of the site’s main layout. The links on the page lead to other pages that continue to show the same problems as the main page.

Sweet Bro and Hella Jeff

Next, there is a page for the first panel of the webcomic “Sweet bro and Hella Jeff.” The page itself is meant to be ironic, yet regardless, it is still a prime example of terrible web design. The site itself has crusty and horribly cut images, consistent use of Comic Sans as its primary font, horrible colors chosen that clash with the background and focus of the page, and the site itself is unruly and uncomfortable to navigate.

Well Designed Websites

Apple

Our first example of a well designed site, is the Apple homepage. The site does a good job of filling the space space that it needs to, in order to showcase its products, and services to the viewer. The text is easy to read, and supplements the large icons, which are the primary focus of the page. The links are easy to find and navigate to look at the specific products that apple has to offer. It is an appealing and easy to navigate site.

Razer

The site for Razer, a computer gaming accessory site has a great layout, color scheme, and composition. It is bright and colorful, which represents the products they are trying to sell. In addition, the Razer logo is very stand out, drawing attention as if to remind the viewer what site they are currently visiting. It does well to use images to showcase their products as well. Despite the site being well put together, I personally do not like the “Gamer” Aesthetic. But that being said, it is still a good site that advertises their products well.
